    Nick Chubb Injury Update: Browns Star Expected to Miss Remainder of Season

    Nick Chubb is a Cleveland Browns NFL running back. A college football player at Georgia, a two-time All-American, and a 2017 national champion. Chubb was drafted by the Browns in the second round in 2018.

    08 Aug 2024, 00:00

    Nick Chubb is a Cleveland Browns NFL running back. A college football player at Georgia, a two-time All-American, and a 2017 national champion. Chubb was drafted by the Browns in the second round in 2018.

    Since joining the NFL, Chubb has been a top running back. He made the Pro Bowl four times and ran for 1,000 yards in his first five seasons. Chubb ran 1,259 yards and scored 8 touchdowns in 2021. He had 20 receptions for 174 yards and one touchdown. Read about Nick Chubb’s injuries below.

    Nick Chubb Injury Update

    Nick Chubb was injured in the second quarter tonight. The injury was not seen on TV, but a graphic replay is available. Chubb was wheeled off the field to the locker room after being unable to stand.

    Prayers up for Nick Chubb. This looks awful in slow-motion pic.twitter.com/H1Zw2NZzjv

    His knee ailment forced the Browns to pull him from the game. Chubb may be done for the season. The team suspects a knee dislocation and ligament tears. After the game, coach Kevin Stefanski said Chubb had a “significant knee injury” and may miss the season. Chubb had a similar knee issue at Georgia in 2015. Losing Chubb is terrible for the Browns. As a top NFL runner, he makes the Pro Bowl every year.

    Jerome Ford and Pierre Strong Jr. are Chubb’s backups. The practice squad adds running back Hassan Hall. Leonard Fournette and Kareem Hunt are free-agent options for the Browns to replace Chubb. Former Browns player Hunt and Rams Cam Akers are trade candidates. The second Browns player to suffer a major injury this season is Chubb. In the season opener, Jack Conklin tore his ACL.

    Nick Chubb’s injury history

    Monday night’s setback happened nearly eight years after Chubb’s horrific left knee injury as a Georgia sophomore. “I remember how it felt when it happened,” Chubb told ESPN in 2016. “My leg tingled. My body moved forward, my leg backwards. I sensed trouble.” Chubb returned the next season and ran for 1,130 yards and five touchdowns after a significant recovery. He surpassed those numbers as a senior, running for 1,345 yards and 15 touchdowns as Georgia won the SEC but lost to Alabama in overtime in the national championship game. The Browns drafted him second in 2018. “Malcolm Gladwell wrote about outliers,” ESPN received from former Georgia sports medical director Ron Courson in 2016. “Nick is unusual. His genes are good. He works hard and is mentally tougher than anyone I’ve seen. I expected his return.” In 2020, Chubb damaged his right MCL and missed four games on injured reserve.

    Nick Chubb Career

    Star Cleveland Browns running back Nick Chubb has had a phenomenal five-season NFL career. He rushed 1,210 times for 6,341 yards. Chubb caught 119 catches for 990 yards in addition to rushing. He’s scored 52 touchdowns, demonstrating his skill. His 2019 and 2020 PFWA All-AFC selections demonstrate his effect on the AFC. His impressive 5.2 yards per carry illustrates how efficient and successful Chubb is as a running back. In a job with constant turnover, this stability is impressive.
