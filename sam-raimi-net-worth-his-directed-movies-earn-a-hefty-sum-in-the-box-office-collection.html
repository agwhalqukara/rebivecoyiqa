    Samuel M. Raimi, professionally known as Sam Raimi, was born on October 23, 1959, in Royal Oak, Michigan. He is an American director, producer, screenwriter, and actor. Raimi rose to fame after creating the Spider-Man movies and the Evil Dead franchise.

    Raimi is also the producer of successful television series, including Hercules: The Legendary Journeys and Xena: Warrior Princess. His movies are best-loved for their flashy and dynamic visual styles, most inspired by comic books. He is one of the prominent producers and directors.

    Along with enormous fame, how much has the famous filmmaker amassed through his filmmaking career? In this article, let’s take a look at Sam Raimi’s net worth and box office collection of his directed movies.

    Sam Raimi Net Worth And Earnings

    Sam Raimi mainly earns from his career as a director and a producer. He became absorbed in filmmaking since childhood. After completing high school at Groves High School, he attended Michigan State University but later dropped out to make “The Evil Dead.” Even without any professional degree, he succeeded in creating the best movies in history and is continuing to work on amazing stuff.

    As a result of his outstanding skills, Raimi not only acquired immense fame but also collected a considerable amount of money. As per Celebrity Net Worth, Sam Raimi’s net worth is $60 million. Now, let’s further learn about how he amassed such a massive amount of money.

    Earning As A Director

    Sam Raimi was born to Celia Barbara and Leonard Ronald Raimi. His brother, Ted Raimi, is a well-known actor. He started directing movies back in 1975 by making Super 8 movies with his friend Bruce Campbell.

    During his college days, he joined his hand with his brother’s roommate, Robert Tapert, and his friend Campbell to make “Within The Woods” and “It’s Murder”. Although these movies were commercially not successful, they captured the interest of investors and viewers, and Sam got offers for investment.

    Raimi decided to invest his all-time into filmmaking and dropped out of college to make The Evil Dead (1981) with a proper financial plan. The movie became a cult hit and Raimi established his career as a director.

    Raimi’s movies started to gain great critical and commercial success as he moved forward in his career. Darkman and the Spider-Man franchise were some of the blockbuster movies he directed. Sam Raimi’s salary for working on these films must have been jaw-dropping.
