    Tim Wakefield’s Baseball Career

    Tim Wakefield was a renowned baseball pitcher who played 19 seasons in Major League Baseball (MLB). He started his career with the Pittsburgh Pirates but is best known for his 17-year tenure as the Boston Red Sox’s longest-tenured player, from 1995 until his retirement in 2012. During his career, Wakefield achieved significant milestones, including his 200th career win against the Toronto Blue Jays on September 13, 2011. He holds the third position in Red Sox franchise history for career victories, following Cy Young and Roger Clemens, with 186 victories. He also holds the record for the most career innings pitched by a Red Sox pitcher (3,006), surpassing Roger Clemens’ previous record.
